Research

My current research has focused on improving abiotic stress tolerance and hence biomass production in trees. I am using a multidisciplinary approach to characterize relevant biochemical mechanisms in poplar, including the identification and functional characterization of major stress proteins and genes. Significant in vivo roles have been elucidated through heterologous expression (bacterial and plant transformation) of major small Heat Shock Proteins (sHSPs) isoforms to protect plants against various abiotic factors acting either individually or simultaneously. In addition, we are transforming poplar with some endogenous key enzymes and hormones to study the effects of their overexpression in growth and biomass woody production.
