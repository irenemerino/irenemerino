Research

My current research has focused on improving abiotic stress tolerance and hence biomass production in trees. I am using a multidisciplinary approach to characterize relevant biochemical mechanisms in poplar, including the identification and functional characterization of major stress proteins and genes. Significant in vivo roles have been elucidated over the past few years through heterologous expression of major small Heat Shock Proteins (sHSPs) to protect plants against various abiotic factors acting either individually or simultaneously. As a result, I have developed several transgenic poplar lines with enhanced growth and in vitro performance.
